I'm Jim. I became fascinated with computers at the age of five when our family purchased a Tandy computer from Radio Shack. Thirty years ago, my Uncle introduced me to [QBasic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QBasic) via the '[gorillas](https://archive.org/details/GorillasQbasic)' game and I've been hooked on programming ever since. When I was in high school I got into [Macromedia flash](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macromedia) and inadvertently started my career as a web developer. I've spent the last 25 years building and launching [SaaS](https://clarus.page) and [mobile](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/air-arizona/id1400658164) products in the [private](https://revolutiones.com/procal) and [public sectors](https://educatorproconnect.org). Today I am co-founder and CTO of [eDNA Explorer](https://ednaexplorer.org/), a bio-informatics platform automating the interpretation of environmental DNA research.

This blog exists as an outlet to start writing again. Writing has been an old habit that I had sadly let go of about 10 years ago. Today my focus lies in leadership, coding, and business.

If I'm not behind a book or a screen you can find me spending time with my family. If I'm not with them — I'm off somewhere riding a bicycle.
